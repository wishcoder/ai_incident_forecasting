Incident ID:012

Title:Firewall-2 Routing All Traffic to App-3

Date and Time:2024-01-20 11:00

Reported By:Alexa Ramirez/Network Operations

Component Affected:Firewall-2, indirectly affecting App-3 and App-4

Incident Description

An incorrect routing rule in Firewall-2 directed all incoming traffic exclusively to App-3, inadvertently leaving App-4 inaccessible and disrupting its service.

Impact Analysis

App-4's inaccessibility from external networks severely impacted its operational capabilities, user access, and service delivery, while App-3 experienced an unexpected surge in traffic.

Root Cause

A configuration error in Firewall-2's routing rules mistakenly directed all incoming traffic to App-3, completely neglecting the designated routes for App-4.

Resolution Steps

1. The misconfiguration was quickly identified through detailed traffic monitoring and analysis.

2. Immediate corrective action was taken to revise and properly implement the routing rules, ensuring balanced traffic distribution to both App-3 and App-4.

3. Access to App-4 was fully restored by 12:30, and operational stability was confirmed through subsequent monitoring.

4. A comprehensive review of firewall configuration processes was initiated to enhance oversight and prevent similar incidents.

Resolved By:Brian Foster/IT Security

Resolution Date and Time:2024-01-20 12:30

Status

Resolved

Lessons Learned

This incident highlighted the critical importance of rigorous pre-implementation review and testing of firewall configurations to ensure accurate traffic routing. It also emphasized the need for continuous monitoring and swift response mechanisms to quickly address any misconfigurations, minimizing impact on service availability and maintaining network integrity.
