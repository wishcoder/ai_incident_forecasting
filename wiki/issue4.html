Incident Details

Incident ID:004

Title:Router-2 is not reachable

Date and Time:2023-05-10 11:47

Reported By:Ethan Wright/Network Operations

Component Affected:Router-2, indirectly affecting Firewall-2, App-3, and App-4

Incident Description

Router-2 became unreachable due to a suspected hardware failure, disrupting external connectivity for App-3 and App-4. This unexpected outage prevented access to critical services and applications.

Impact Analysis

The loss of Router-2 connectivity halted all external traffic to App-3 and App-4, significantly impacting user operations and service delivery. The disruption highlighted the critical dependency on Router-2 for external connectivity to these applications.

Root Cause

Power failure caused Router-2 to shut down unexpectedly. Subsequent hardware diagnostics confirmed a power supply unit (PSU) failure as the specific cause.

Resolution Steps

1. Immediate notification to the hardware team for diagnostics.

2. Temporary routing adjustments made to divert traffic through Router-1 while Router-2 was under investigation, ensuring minimal service disruption.

3. Hardware fault identified as a PSU failure, and Router-2 was replaced at 14:30.

4. After replacement, comprehensive testing was conducted to ensure Router-2's full operational capacity.

5. Router-2 was brought back online, and normal service for App-3 and App-4 was restored by 15:00.

Resolved By:Fiona Kelley/Hardware Support Team

Resolution Date and Time:2023-05-10 15:00

Status

Resolved

Lessons Learned

This incident underlined the importance of having a robust hardware monitoring and failure detection system in place, particularly for critical network components like routers. It also highlighted the need for a quicker hardware replacement process and the effectiveness of having a contingency plan, such as traffic rerouting capabilities, to maintain service continuity during critical hardware failures. Additionally, the incident emphasized the value of regular power supply unit (PSU) checks and replacements as part of routine maintenance to prevent similar outages.
